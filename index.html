Shuxing Xuan

I am currently a fourth-year PhD candidate at the School of Automation, University of Electronic Science and Technology of China, and am preparing to commence my postdoctoral research. I received the B.E. degree in Automation in 2019 and the M.E. degree in Control Theory and Control Engineering in 2022 from Lanzhou University of Technology. My early research interests focused on deep learning and image processing, while my current research is centered on cooperative control of multi-agent systems and the extension of reinforcement learning methodologies, with particular emphasis on distributed control, optimal decision-making, safety-constrained control, and the theoretical analysis and engineering applications of swarm intelligence systems. During my academic training, I received multiple awards in mathematical modeling competitions and was honored with the National Scholarship for Doctoral Students. My future research will further focus on the modeling, learning, and control of swarm intelligence systems and their applications in complex engineering systems.

Research

I'm interested in computer vision, deep learning, generative AI, and image processing. Most of my research is about inferring the physical world (shape, motion, color, light, etc) from images, usually with radiance fields. Some papers are highlighted.

Radiance Meshes for Volumetric Reconstruction
Alexander Mai, Trevor Hedstrom, George Kopanas,
Janne Kontkanen, Falko Kuester, Jonathan T. Barron
arXiv, 2025
project page / arXiv

Parameterizing a scene with a Delaunay tetrahedralization and a neural field yields a scene representation that is accurate, fast to render, easy to edit, and backwards-compatible.

NExF: Learning Neural Exposure Fields for View Synthesis

NeurIPS, 2025
project page / arXiv

Learning a neural field that optimizes exposure for each 3D point enables high-quality 3D-consistent view synthesis despite extreme exposure variation during capture.

Bolt3D: Generating 3D Scenes in Seconds
